What are regular expressions?

Regular Expressions are a sequence of characters that are used to search for substrings ("matches") in strings. They are mainly for use in pattern matching.

Why would I use them?

Regular Expressions have lots of uses in scripting. You could use them to replace all occurrences of certain strings in a file or test whether a particular input was submitted correctly (Such as an email address). What does a regular expression look like? A regular expression is usually a set of symbols and characters between two forward slashes. For example,

/[abc]+/
A simple one matching any string that begins with 'a', 'b', or 'c'

/^[A-Za-z0-9_\.-]+@[A-Za-z0-9_\.-]+[A-Za-z0-9_][A-Za-z0-9_]$/
A regular expression for matching an email address (a little more complex)
